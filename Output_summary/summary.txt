The appellants, Messrs Abdul Karim and Brothers, owned, along with certain other properties, three mills with bungalows and chawls at Ambernath in Thana District and the Bobbin Factory at Tardeo in Bombay. They had migrated to Pakistan, these properties were declared by a notification dated 12 9 1951 issued under section 7 of the Act as evacuee property and became vested in the respondent as the Custodian for the State. On 30 8 1952 the respondent entered into an agreement with them, Exhibit A, which is, as aptly characterised by learned counsel for the appellants of a composite character, consisting of three distinct matters. There was, firstly, a demise under which the mills and the factory in question were leased to them for a period of five years on the terms and conditions set out therein. Secondly, there was a sale of the stock of raw materials, unsold finished goods, spare parts, cars, trucks and other movables, with elaborate provisions for the determination and payment of the price therefor in 979 due course. The respondent issued a notice to them, pursuant to section 12 of the (XXXI of 1950) Act, and called upon them to show cause why the lease should not be cancelled and why they should be evicted. On the first question, the respondent held that section 12 applied only to the premises of the deceased and not to properties of the evaCuee, and that the notice, Exhibit C, was ultra vires the powers of the Custodian under section 10 of the said Act. He held that Section 12 applied to the deceased, and Section 10 of said Act to the properties, and the respondent had no jurisdiction to issue the notice in question under section 12.
